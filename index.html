A 10-day social canvassing mission in Iowa

Yang Party is assembling high energy humans to paint Ames red white and blue for Andrew Yang

Andrew is running a 21st century campaign, and this calls for 21st century canvassing. Knocking on doors is important work for reaching voters. However, many people find it unnatural. Making matters worse, some voters refuse to open their doors, and many college students live in dorms where we can't even knock.

Yang Party advocates a new form of canvassing, where

canvassers are paid a basic income of $33/day for each day they party with the group

. With these funds, we can spend time and money at local businesses in public spaces, where people are naturally more open to conversations. We can leverage the energy of the Yang Gang by lighting up coffee shops, restaurants, bars, yoga studios, college campuses and more. Instead of cold approaching Iowans at their homes, let them see how much fun we are and connect with them as humans first

This idea was inspired by Yang Week and Sorority of Yang. We saw first hand how well our message is received in a pub filled with warm, inclusive people wearing MATH hats. We're launching a movement to show voters our powerful platform, rather than merely telling them about it. The Freedom Dividend is the most tangible policy ever proposed: so let's give people money to spend at local businesses in Iowa. We believe there is no more effective way to reach with Iowans

As we onboard the local population to the Yang Gang, they too will be able to opt in to the Yang Party dividend, so long as they indeed spend all day with us keeping the party alive and spreading the word to their friends and family!

How do we pay for it?

Between the dividend and hotel rooms, each person will cost about $420 per 7 days. The creator of Yang Party will seed the fund with money to support 10 partiers for one week. After we receive 70 days worth of commitments, we will begin accepting donations. We continue to be astonished by the fundraising abilities of the Yang Gang, and we're open to this becoming as big as the people can make it.
